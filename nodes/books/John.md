# John

The Gospel of sevens and signs. Seven signs demonstrating who Jesus is
(Cana, nobleman's son, Bethesda, five thousand, walking on the sea,
blind man, Lazarus) and seven predicated "I AM" sayings declaring it
(bread of life, light of the world, door, good shepherd, resurrection,
way-truth-life, true vine). The prologue (1:1–18) declares the incarnation;
the epilogue (chap. 21) seals the Evangelist's testimony.

Key signature: John 20:30–31 — *"these are written, that ye might believe
that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God."*

John 21 carries a full textual-evidence panel (Westcott 1880, Godet 1886,
Bullinger 1895, Augustine on 153 fish, Jerome on Oppian's fish species) —
verdict: Authentic. Contrast with Mark 16, where genuine textual challenge
exists; John 21 is present in every known manuscript tradition.
