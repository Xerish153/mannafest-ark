# Luke

Luke's "orderly account" (1:3) follows Jesus from Bethlehem through the
long travel narrative (9:51–19:27) to Jerusalem and the Emmaus road. Luke
gives us the infancy narratives, the Magnificat and Benedictus and Nunc
dimittis, the Nazareth manifesto (Isa 61 read in the synagogue), and the
great parables of seeking (Good Samaritan, Prodigal Son) in the travel
narrative. Luke is also the Gospel of the Holy Spirit — a theological
spine carrying forward into Acts.

Key signature: Luke 19:10 — *"the Son of man is come to seek and to save
that which was lost."*
