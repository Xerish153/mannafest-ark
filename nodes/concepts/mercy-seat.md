# Mercy Seat (Kapporet → Hilastērion)

The gold lid of the [[ark-of-covenant]], with two cherubim of hammered gold facing each other over it (Ex 25:17–22). God's manifest presence dwells *"between the two cherubim which are upon the ark of the testimony"* (Ex 25:22). The High Priest sprinkles blood here once a year on Yom Kippur (Lev 16).

**The single most explicit OT-NT terminological identification in scripture:**

- Hebrew **kapporet** (from **kaphar** — to cover / to atone)
- LXX Greek **hilastērion** (ἱλαστήριον)
- **Romans 3:25** uses the *exact same Greek word* of Christ: *"whom God hath set forth to be a propitiation [hilastērion] through faith in his blood."* Paul deliberately identifies Christ as the Mercy Seat itself — the place where God's wrath is satisfied and atonement is made.

Featured commentary: John Owen, *The Death of Death in the Death of Christ* (1647, fully PD). The [[project-314]] interpretation surfaces as a discoverable reading at the framework level — not endorsed on-site, Missler cited as metadata only.
