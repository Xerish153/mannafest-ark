# Virgo — Bethulah / Almah

The constellation Bullinger places first in his proposed gospel-in-the-stars sequence (Bullinger starts at Virgo, not Aries — itself a contested choice). Ancient Hebrew name *Bethulah* or *Almah* (virgin). Bullinger's associated decan *Coma* is glossed as "the desired one."

**Bullinger's reading:** the promised seed — Genesis 3:15's coming deliverer born of a woman, prefigured in constellation form.

**Critical echo:** Coma's "desired one" reading depends on a contested etymology that [[faulkner-gospel-in-stars-critique]] argues was selected to fit the gospel narrative. The broader transmission-chain critique (Albumazar dependency) applies here as elsewhere.
