# The Great Isaiah Scroll (1QIsaⁱ)

A complete Hebrew manuscript of the book of Isaiah, discovered in 1947 by Bedouin shepherds in Qumran Cave 1, paleographically dated c. 125 BC and radiocarbon-dated consistently. Currently on permanent display at the Shrine of the Book in Jerusalem; the full scroll is publicly accessible in the [Israel Museum's Digital Dead Sea Scrolls archive](https://dss.collections.imj.org.il/isaiah).

The [[Suffering-Servant]] passage (Isaiah 52:13–53:12) appears in column XLIV of the scroll as continuous prose — no chapter break (the 1227 AD Langton chapter divisions hadn't been invented yet). The 1QIsaⁱ text of this passage is substantially identical to the Masoretic Text used today, demonstrating near-perfect transmission across approximately 1,000 years.

What the scroll settles: a complete Hebrew copy of Isaiah's prophecy existed **at least 150 years before the crucifixion**, preserved by Jewish scribes with no knowledge of Christ. The text Christians point to as predicting Jesus is verifiably pre-Christian.
