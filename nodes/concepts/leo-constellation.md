# Leo — Aryeh

The constellation at the climax of Bullinger's proposed gospel-in-the-stars sequence. Hebrew *Aryeh* (lion). Bullinger reads Leo as the triumphant king — the Lion of the tribe of Judah arriving in victory.

**Biblical anchor (genuinely present):** Revelation 5:5 — *"Behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book."* The Lion-of-Judah Christological reading is itself biblical.

**Contested claim:** whether the constellation Leo specifically encodes that reading by ancient Hebrew design is the point at issue. The biblical identification of Christ as Lion of Judah is unproblematic; the claim that Leo-the-constellation was named by antediluvian patriarchs to prefigure that identification is the reading critics challenge.

**Editorial slot eligible:** this is the place where the gospel-in-the-stars reading presses its strongest genuinely biblical connection — founder editorial note appropriate.
