# Jeconiah's Curse

**Jeremiah 22:24–30** pronounces judgment on Coniah (Jeconiah / Jehoiachin), Judah's second-to-last king before the Babylonian exile: *"no man of his seed shall prosper, sitting upon the throne of David, and ruling any more in Judah."* The line is cursed from the throne — yet **Matthew 1:11–12** traces Jesus' legal genealogy directly through Jeconiah to Joseph.

The reconciliation is the foundation of the [[Genealogies-of-Christ]] feature page's central argument:

- [[Matthew's genealogy]] records **Joseph's legal line** (Jewish adoption law transfers throne-rights without requiring biological descent).
- [[Luke's genealogy]] records **Mary's biological line** through David's son Nathan — untainted by Jeconiah's curse.
- The [[Virgin-birth]] delivers a Davidic Messiah who is *both* legal heir (via Joseph) and biological descendant (via Mary) — structurally required, not decoratively miraculous.
