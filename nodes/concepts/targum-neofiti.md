# Targum Neofiti

An Aramaic paraphrase (*targum*) of the Pentateuch in the Palestinian tradition, preserving interpretive glosses that circulated in first-century (and earlier) Jewish exegesis. The manuscript itself was rediscovered in the Vatican Library in 1949 after being mis-catalogued for centuries as *Targum Onkelos*. Though the extant manuscript dates to the 16th century, the interpretive traditions it preserves are demonstrably earlier — many predate the Christian era.

**Targum Neofiti on Genesis 3:15** reads the verse messianically, preserving the *"seed of the woman"* as a singular redeemer who crushes the serpent. *"Targum Jonathan"* on the same verse goes further, glossing: *"And it shall come to pass that when the sons of the woman study the Torah diligently and keep the commandments, they will aim at you (the serpent) and smite your head; and when the sons of the woman forsake the commandments, you will wound them in the heel. Nevertheless for them there will be a remedy, and for you there will be no remedy; and they shall appoint peace in the end of days, in the days of the Messiah."*

These Targumic readings demonstrate that the **messianic interpretation of Genesis 3:15 is not a Christian innovation** retroactively applied to a Jewish text — it is an older Jewish interpretive thread that the Christian reading inherits.
