# The Veil (Paroket)

Woven of blue, purple, scarlet, and fine linen, with **cherubim embroidered on it** (Ex 26:31). The same beings that guard the way to the tree of life in Eden (Gen 3:24) are woven into the veil guarding access to God in the Tabernacle. The veil is, in one reading, a recurring reminder of the Eden separation.

Matthew 27:51 records the veil of the temple torn in two **from the top to the bottom** at Christ's death — top-to-bottom signaling divine, not human, action (a veil ~60 ft high in the Second Temple could not be humanly torn from the top).

**Hebrews 10:19–20** identifies the veil as Christ's flesh: *"by a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh."* Torn flesh = open access. The Eden separation is reversed.
