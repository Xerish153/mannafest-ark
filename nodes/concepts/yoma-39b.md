# Yoma 39b — The Forty-Year Witness

The Mishnah (*Yoma* 6:8) preserves a Yom Kippur ritual in which a **scarlet cord** was tied to the Temple door before the scapegoat was sent into the wilderness. When the scapegoat had borne Israel's sins away and the atonement was accepted, the cord **turned white** (per [[Isaiah 1:18]] — *"though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow"*). The whitening was read as the tangible sign that the atonement ritual had succeeded.

The **Babylonian Talmud, tractate Yoma, folio 39b** records that *"for forty years before the destruction of the Temple"* — destroyed by Rome in AD 70, so from **approximately AD 30 onward** — the scarlet cord **ceased to turn white**. The sign failed. Year after year. Until the Temple itself fell.

AD 30 is the widely-held date of the [[Crucifixion]].

This is preserved in a Jewish text, transmitted by Jewish scribes with every interpretive reason *not* to preserve a detail that could be read Christologically. They preserved it anyway. The [[Scarlet-Thread]] feature page treats this as the hinge of the canon's atonement typology — the ritual sign of atonement failed in the year Christians say the true atonement was made.
