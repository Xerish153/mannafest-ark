# The Gospel in the Stars (Seiss, 1882)

Joseph A. Seiss, *The Gospel in the Stars; or, Prímeval Astronomy* (Philadelphia: E. Claxton & Co., 1882). Lutheran clergyman. The earlier and, in some ways, more rigorous treatment of the constellation-gospel thesis that [[bullinger-witness-of-the-stars]] would systematize eleven years later.

**Public domain** — cited freely on MannaFest per Doctrine A (≤50 words direct quote, one quote per context, attribution visible).

**Sometimes diverges from Bullinger** on specific constellation readings. MannaFest's Mazzaroth page cites both where they agree and notes the divergence in the constellation drilldowns where they don't.
