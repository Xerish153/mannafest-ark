# The Witness of the Stars (Bullinger, 1893)

E.W. Bullinger, *The Witness of the Stars* (London: self-published, 1893). Anglican clergyman and dispensationalist; Bullinger's systematic compilation of the constellation-gospel reading. Credits [[seiss-gospel-in-the-stars]] (1882) and Frances Rolleston's *Mazzaroth* (1862, PD) as antecedents, and builds a constellation-by-constellation, decan-by-decan case that the twelve signs encode the gospel of Christ.

**Public domain** — cited freely on MannaFest per Doctrine A (≤50 words direct quote, one quote per context, attribution visible).

**Critical caveat the page surfaces honestly** ([[faulkner-gospel-in-stars-critique]]): Bullinger's detailed constellation-figure descriptions trace not to ancient Hebrew sources but to Albumazar (Abu Ma'shar al-Balkhi, d. 886 AD) and other medieval Islamic / European astronomical compilations. Readers can verify this by checking Bullinger's own footnotes.
