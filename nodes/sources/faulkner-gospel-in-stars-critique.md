# Faulkner — Is the Gospel in the Stars? (2015)

Danny Faulkner, *"Is the Gospel in the Stars?"* **Answers Research Journal** / **Journal of Creation**, 2015. Faulkner is a creationist astronomer (PhD Indiana 1989), affiliated with Answers in Genesis. A voice sympathetic to biblical authority and young-earth creationism, yet sharply critical of the [[bullinger-witness-of-the-stars]] and [[seiss-gospel-in-the-stars]] thesis on specific historical grounds.

**Primary critical claim:** Bullinger's detailed constellation-figure descriptions — decan figures, specific imagery, secondary star-grouping names — trace to **Abu Ma'shar al-Balkhi (Albumazar, d. 886 AD)** and other medieval Islamic and European astronomical compilations, not to ancient Hebrew tradition. The transmission chain Bullinger claims (antediluvian Hebrew revelation → distortion → recovery via preserved sources) doesn't match the actual chain that historical inquiry can trace (Babylonian → Greek → Arabic → medieval European → 19th-century sources Bullinger consulted).
