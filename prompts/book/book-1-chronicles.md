## Identity

A post-exilic retelling of Israel's story through the Davidic line — genealogies from Adam to Saul, then David's reign reframed around temple preparation. The priestly history answering the exile's question: what does it mean to return?

## Framework (Tier 2 template)

- Historical setting: post-exilic, c. 450–400 BC; traditional attribution to Ezra.
- Literary structure: genealogies (1–9) + David's reign reframed temple-ward (10–29).
- Theological contribution: temple orientation; Davidic covenant extended (ch. 17); the Chronicler's hope for the returnee community.
