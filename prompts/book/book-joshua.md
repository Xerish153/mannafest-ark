## Identity

Entry into the Land. Israel crosses the Jordan, takes the Land through three conquest campaigns (central / southern / northern), receives tribal allotments, and renews covenant at Shechem. The hinge between Torah's promises and the historical books' record of those promises unfolding.

## Framework sections (depth 2)

- From Moses to Joshua — "be strong and courageous" (Josh 1:6, 7, 9) + the Law never departing from Joshua's mouth (1:8).
- The Conquest debate (§7.9): maximalist historical / minimalist archaeological / social-conflict / biblical-theological framings — site surfaces, does not adjudicate.
- Cities of refuge + Levitical cities — the distribution that saturates the Land with Torah teaching and judicial protection.
- Shechem covenant renewal (ch. 24) — ANE treaty form; the witness stone; the charge that closes the book.
