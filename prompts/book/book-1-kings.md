## Identity

From Solomon's glory to the divided kingdom — then Elijah's confrontation with Ahab and Jezebel dominates the second half. The Davidic promise unfolding through temple glory, then beginning to unravel as idolatry and political fragmentation take hold.

## Framework sections (depth 2)

- Solomon's temple (6–8) — fulfilment of 2 Sam 7's promise of a son who builds the house; dedication prayer as covenant-renewal liturgy.
- The division of the kingdom (11–12) — Solomon's three sins + Ahijah's announcement + Rehoboam at Shechem + Jeroboam's calves.
- Elijah's ministry (17–19, 21) — drought, Zarephath, Carmel (signature verse), Horeb, Naboth. The prophet against the apostate crown.
- The divided-kingdom regnal formula — cross-dated accession years; northern kings uniformly evil; southern kings evaluated by Davidic fidelity.
