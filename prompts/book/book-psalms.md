## Identity

The canon's longest book. 150 Psalms edited into five doxology-bookended books composed across a millennium (Mosaic Ps 90 → post-exilic Ps 126). A "Torah of David"; the Bible's longest continuous liturgical-devotional corpus. Featured voice: Spurgeon's Treasury of David (3,000+ pages, 20-year labor, shipped Batch 8.1).

## Framework sections (depth 2)

- Fivefold Psalter as "Torah of David" — doxology bookends, Midrash Tehillim parallel to the Pentateuch, distinct theological centers per book.
- Authorship and attribution — David (73), Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1), Heman + Ethan, ~50 anonymous.
- Genre and form — Gunkel's six categories; lament as the most common; penitential subset; imprecatory subset (§7.9 without adjudication).
- Pss 1 and 2 as intentional introduction — Torah-delight + messianic enthronement as wisdom-and-messiah frame.
- The messianic throughline — 6 anchor Psalms + 7 contextual references; NT citation patterns.
- The Psalter in Christian liturgy — monastic weekly recitation; Reformation metrical Psalters; Spurgeon's Treasury as featured-voice corpus.
