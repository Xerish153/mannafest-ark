# Book — 1 Samuel

## Identity

The transition from judges to monarchy. Samuel rises as prophet-judge; Israel demands a king; Saul rises and is rejected; David is anointed in obscurity and flees across the wilderness. The prophetic-judicial-royal handoff.

## Framework sections (depth 2)

- Hannah's song and the book's theological grammar (2:1–10) — the Magnificat's OT precursor.
- The demand for a king (ch. 8) — Deut 17:14–20 covenant-kingship framework put to the test.
- The heart, not the outward appearance (16:7) — debated "after God's own heart" (13:14, Acts 13:22); chosen-by-God vs morally-superior-to-Saul readings surfaced without adjudication.
- David in the wilderness (19–30) — Pss 34, 52, 54, 56, 57, 59, 63, 142 superscribed to this period.
