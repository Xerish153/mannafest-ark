## Identity

Love poetry in eight chapters — the Bible's most surprising book. Chiastic structure with 4:16–5:1 as the center. A bride and bridegroom alternating in dialogue. Three interpretive schools (literal / allegorical / typological) surfaced without adjudication per §7.9.

## Framework (Tier 2 template)

- Literary structure: opening songs — courtship (1–2); wedding and consummation (3–5); mature love and close (6–8). Chiastic center at 4:16–5:1.
- Three interpretive schools: literal (Protestant majority — Calvin, modern Reformed, Luther's later position); allegorical (Origen, Bernard of Clairvaux, Spurgeon — Christ + Church / the individual believer); typological synthesis (literal primary + messianic secondary via Eph 5:31–32 — modern Reformed).
- Canon role: part of the Megillot; read at Passover in Jewish tradition; a devotional and (per school) allegorical anchor in Christian tradition.
