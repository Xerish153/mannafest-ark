## Identity

Two kingdoms on parallel but diverging arcs. Elisha's ministry dominates chs 1–13; the northern fall in 722 BC; Hezekiah and the Assyrian crisis; Manasseh, Josiah, and the Babylonian crisis; the fall of Jerusalem in 586 BC; the Jehoiachin coda that keeps the Davidic covenant alive into the exile.

## Framework sections (depth 2)

- Elisha's ministry — double portion; Naaman the Syrian (Luke 4:27); the floating axe head; the post-mortem miracle (13:21). Miracle clusters prefigure Christ.
- The fall of Samaria (ch. 17) — why Israel fell; the Samaritan origins (17:24–41 → John 4); Deut 28 covenant curses enacted.
- Hezekiah and Sennacherib (18–20) — Nehushtan destroyed; 185,000 Assyrian dead; Isaiah intervenes.
- Josiah and the fall of Jerusalem (22–25) — Book of the Law found; reform can't undo Manasseh's judgment; three-wave deportation; Jehoiachin released (25:27–30).
