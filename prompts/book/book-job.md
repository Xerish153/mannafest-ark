## Identity

The canonical wisdom-literature interrogation of undeserved suffering. A prose prologue and epilogue enclose three cycles of dialogue whose third round collapses — Zophar silent. Elihu's late intervention precedes the whirlwind theophany, and resolution is theophanic, not argumentative.

## Framework sections (depth 2)

- Prologue's heavenly court (Job 1–2) — *ha-satan* in the divine council; two tests; Job's integrity under both. Cross-link into future Satan-profile page (parking-lot).
- The dialogue's theological collapse — friends' rigid retribution theology vs. Job's knowledge of his own innocence; third-cycle structural collapse as argument.
- The *go'el* and the redeemer (19:25–27) — Hebrew kinsman-redeemer; Calvin and Henry's Christological reading vs. Jewish restraint, §7.9.
- The whirlwind theophany and the book's resolution — YHWH reorients rather than answers; twofold repentance; doubled restoration.
