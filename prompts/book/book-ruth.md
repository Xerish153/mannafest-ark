## Identity

A Moabite widow's loyalty to her Israelite mother-in-law during the days of the judges. Four chapters, four acts, chiastically arranged — emptying and filling (chs 1 & 4), gleaning and threshing floor (chs 2 & 3). The closing genealogy is the book's real payload.

## Framework sections (depth 2)

- The kinsman-redeemer (*go'el*) — Lev 25:25–55 + Deut 25:5–10 legal backdrop; Boaz's dual role as redeemer of land + levirate husband; Christological typology.
- Ruth the Moabite — Deut 23:3 exclusion; Matt 1:5 inclusion; the narrative's theological provocation.
- Naomi's return and restoration — the name-change (''call me Mara'') and the closing reversal.
- Ruth in the messianic genealogy — tenth-generation placement as theological argument about Gentile inclusion.
