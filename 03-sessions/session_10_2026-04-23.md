## AI-draft source attribution

Each of the 87 Pauline chapter summaries synthesizes from:

- Matthew Henry, *An Exposition of the Old and New Testament* (1706–14)
- John Calvin, *Commentaries on Paul's Epistles* (1539–55, tr. Owen/
  Beveridge/Pringle)
- Robert Jamieson, A. R. Fausset, David Brown, *Commentary Critical
  and Explanatory on the Whole Bible* (1871)
- John Wesley, *Explanatory Notes upon the New Testament* (1754)

No modern or copyrighted scholar's commentary was reproduced in these
drafts. The ≤50-word fair-use rule (Doctrine E) did not apply — these
are paraphrases synthesizing multiple PD sources, with no continuous
quotation of any one source. AI may summarize and synthesize cited
human sources per Vision v2 §4.1; it may not introduce theological
claims that aren't in the sources.
