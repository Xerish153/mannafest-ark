## 1. Book identity

1 Peter is the apostle Peter's letter to Christians scattered across five Roman provinces of Asia Minor (Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, Bithynia — 1:1) who are suffering for the name of Christ. The date is commonly placed c. 62–64 AD, before Peter's martyrdom under Nero; the location is "Babylon" (5:13), which the earliest Christian tradition understands as cryptogram for Rome.

The letter's central pastoral move is to recast suffering as participation in Christ's pattern. 1 Pet 2:9 announces the identity of the church — "ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people" — directly echoing Ex 19:6 language applied to Israel, now extended to the predominantly-Gentile Asian churches. 1 Pet 2:21–25 is the NT's most sustained verbatim echo of Isaiah 53, walking the reader through the Suffering Servant passage as both atonement and model. Peter concludes with the famous "casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you" (5:7) and the metaphor of the devil "as a roaring lion" (5:8).

1 Pet 3:18–22 is the letter's most-debated passage — Christ "preached unto the spirits in prison" after the crucifixion. Patristic and Reformation readings differ significantly; §7.9 discipline applies.

## 2. Signature verse rationale

1 Pet 2:9 is one of the NT's great identity-statements for the church — "chosen generation, royal priesthood, holy nation." Applies Ex 19:6 to the New Covenant community. Frequently memorized, widely preached, theologically foundational.

## 3. Stat strip

- **Author:** Peter the apostle (Simon Peter), assisted by Silvanus (Silas, per 5:12)
- **Date:** c. 62–64 AD (before Peter's traditional martyrdom under Nero c. 64–68)
- **Audience:** Christians in five Roman provinces of Asia Minor — predominantly Gentile-background believers
- **Theme:** living hope in suffering; the identity of the church as God's people
- **Distinguishing notes:** "Babylon" (5:13) as likely cryptogram for Rome; sustained Isa 53 echo at 2:21–25; debated "spirits in prison" passage at 3:18–22

## 9. Known contested points

- **1 Pet 3:18–22 "spirits in prison."** Four major readings: (a) Christ preached through Noah to pre-flood generation (Augustine; classical Reformed); (b) Christ descended to fallen angels between death and resurrection to proclaim victory (Lutheran / some Catholic); (c) Christ preached to righteous dead in Hades to lead them out ("harrowing of hell"); (d) Christ as the pre-incarnate Spirit preached to disobedient humans in Noah's generation. §7.9 discipline — surface all four; site does not adjudicate.
- **Pseudonymous-Peter proposal.** Some critical scholarship argues 1 Peter's Greek style is too polished for a Galilean fisherman, proposing pseudonymous later authorship. Traditional resolution: Silvanus as amanuensis (5:12 "by Silvanus ... I have written") accounts for Greek sophistication. Surface; don't adjudicate at hub level.
- **Location "Babylon" (5:13).** Rome (patristic consensus) vs. literal Babylon in Mesopotamia vs. Babylon in Egypt. Rome is strongly majority. Note.
- **Date — Neronian or late-Neronian.** Dating depends partly on whether references to suffering (4:12 "fiery trial") fit official Neronian persecution (c. 64+) or earlier unofficial pressure. Surface briefly.
