## Page intent

The Bible's oldest and longest prophecy, traced through 12 progressive narrowings of the messianic line across 4,000+ years of scripture. The feature page demonstrates that the identity of the Messiah was progressively revealed — each stop eliminating candidates — until only one person could fulfill it. ## Depth 2 — Framework

Three framework sections, each a card cluster:

### 2.1 The pattern that narrows

Short explanatory section on how progressive revelation works in Hebrew scripture. Each covenant moment eliminates candidates rather than adding them. Field starts as "all humanity" and ends at "one person." This narrowing is itself evidence of a single guiding author across 1500+ years of composition.

Card elements: brief intro + a "probability" card noting that the odds of a random person fulfilling even 8 of these 12 narrowings is vanishingly small (cite Peter Stoner, *Science Speaks*, PD reasoning).

### 2.2 The seed of the woman

The grammatical oddity in Genesis 3:15. Hebrew genealogies trace through the father (e.g., "the seed of Abraham," "the seed of David") — but Genesis 3:15 is the only place where scripture speaks of "the seed of the woman." This anticipates Matthew 1's careful genealogy traced through Joseph's line *legally* but noting that Jesus was born of Mary specifically ("of whom was born Jesus"), and Luke 3's genealogy traced through Mary's line biologically.

Include: Bereshit Rabbah 23 (Jewish rabbinic commentary, compiled 300–500 AD, preserving earlier traditions). Jewish interpreters before the Christian era already read Genesis 3:15 messianically — "Eve had respect to that seed which is to come from another place, and who is he? He is the Messiah the King." Tradition tag: Jewish.

### 2.3 The singular in Galatians

Paul's grammatical argument in Galatians 3:16 — the Hebrew zera (זֶרַע) is grammatically singular, like the English "sheep." Paul argues that the promises made to "Abraham's seed" always referred to one specific descendant, not the collective nation. This is the content currently leaking onto depth 1; moved here where it belongs.

Include: note that Jewish scholars have contested Paul's reading historically, but Paul's Hebrew grammar is defensible — zera can carry either collective or individual force, and the messianic reading is already present in rabbinic sources (see §2.2). Tradition tag: Evangelical primary, Jewish secondary.

### 2.4 The enmity fulfilled

Revelation 12:1–5 brings the Genesis 3:15 enmity to its cosmic conclusion. The woman crowned with stars, the dragon (the serpent), the male child who will rule with a rod of iron — all three characters from Genesis 3:15 resurface at the end of scripture.

Frame as: the Bible begins with a promise and ends with its fulfillment. Visual note: render a small two-panel card comparing Gen 3:15 side-by-side with Rev 12:5.
