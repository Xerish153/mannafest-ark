## 1. Book identity

Obadiah is the shortest OT book — a single chapter of 21 verses — and the canon's most concentrated oracle against Edom. Edom, the descendants of Esau, stood by (or actively participated) when Jerusalem fell. Obadiah announces that the ancient fraternal betrayal (Esau-and-Jacob) will be judged: Edom's mountain pride will not save it from the Day of the LORD that comes on all nations.

The book's force is in its brevity. Two themes carry it: the specific judgment on Edom for its treatment of fallen Judah, and the universal Day of the LORD on "all the heathen" (1:15). The closing verse (1:21) looks forward: "saviours shall come up on mount Zion to judge the mount of Esau; and the kingdom shall be the LORD's."

## 2. Signature verse rationale

Obad 1:15 — "For the day of the LORD is near upon all the heathen: as thou hast done, it shall be done unto thee" — captures both the Edom-specific judgment and the universal Day-of-the-LORD framework in a single line. The only Obadiah verse most readers know; anchoring the hub here makes the book memorable.

## 3. Stat strip

- **Author:** Obadiah ("servant of YHWH" — the name is common; identity uncertain)
- **Date:** disputed — either c. 845 BC (after Jehoram's reign, per some early traditions) or c. 586–585 BC (immediately after Jerusalem's fall, strongest internal evidence). Site default: post-586 dating.
- **Audience:** Judah after Edom's betrayal
- **Theme:** judgment on the proud, restoration of Zion
- **Distinguishing notes:** shortest OT book; single chapter; concentrated oracle genre; the Edom-Jacob fraternal theme deepens Genesis's narrative arc
