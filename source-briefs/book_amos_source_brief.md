## 1. Book identity

Amos is a Tekoan shepherd and tender of sycamore-fig trees (Amos 7:14) sent north from Judah to prophesy against Israel in the prosperous reign of Jeroboam II (c. 760–750 BC). He is the book of social justice in the prophetic corpus — relentless on the oppression of the poor, the corruption of worship, and the self-satisfaction of the wealthy. God through Amos says: "let justice roll down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream" (5:24).

The book opens with oracles against the surrounding nations (chs 1–2), narrows to Judah, and lands on Israel herself — the reader realizes the net closes on God's own people. Five visions (chs 7–8) present Israel's judgment in escalating images. The book closes (9:11–15) with a restoration promise — the Davidic booth restored — quoted at the Jerusalem council (Acts 15:16–17) as fulfilled in Gentile inclusion.

## 2. Signature verse rationale

Amos 5:24 captures the book's central ethical concern with cultural staying-power (MLK cited it in "I Have a Dream"). Short enough to render in full. Anchors the social-justice reading of OT prophecy.

## 3. Stat strip

- **Author:** Amos (a herdsman of Tekoa, Judah)
- **Date:** c. 760–750 BC (reigns of Uzziah of Judah and Jeroboam II of Israel; two years before the earthquake, Amos 1:1)
- **Audience:** the northern kingdom in its prosperous apex — prophet from Judah, audience in Samaria
- **Theme:** justice, righteousness, and the judgment the wealthy do not see coming
- **Distinguishing notes:** shepherd-prophet, not from the prophetic schools (7:14); structural pattern of oracles-against-nations narrowing to Israel

## 5. Chapter summary guidance

- Chs 1–2: oracles against eight entities (Damascus, Gaza, Tyre, Edom, Ammon, Moab, Judah, Israel) — the funnel narrows. Note the rhetorical structure.
- Chs 3–6: judgment oracles against Israel proper; woe sayings; warning against the "day of the LORD" being the light it is not.
- Chs 7–8: five visions (locusts, fire, plumb line, summer fruit, LORD at the altar). Summaries note each vision's symbolism.
- Ch 9: final judgment + restoration promise. Flag 9:11–12 and the Acts 15 NT citation.

## 9. Known contested points

- **The earthquake (Amos 1:1)** — dated to c. 760 BC by archaeology (confirmed at Hazor); Amos's ministry is dated relative to it. Minor.
- **Amos 7:14 tense.** "I am no prophet" (present-tense reading) or "I was no prophet" (past-tense, meaning Amos is now a prophet called-out-of-secular-life). Both readings surface in PD commentary.
- **Amos 9:11–12 fulfillment.** Acts 15:16–17 applies the passage to Gentile inclusion in the church. Dispensational readings sometimes argue for a literal future Davidic-kingdom fulfillment alongside. Present both; don't adjudicate.
